1. Overview of Vael

Vael is a free, open-source application designed to help families manage their finances together. Key characteristics:

  • Zero-knowledge architecture: All financial data is encrypted on your device before any transmission

3. Data Privacy and Security

See our Privacy Policy for complete details. In summary:

  • Your data is yours: All financial information stays on your device, encrypted
  • We cannot access it: No servers, no telemetry, no analytics
  • Google Drive sync: Data is encrypted with AES-256-GCM before leaving your device. Google cannot read it
  • Passphrase responsibility: You are responsible for your encryption passphrase. If lost, data cannot be recovered
  • Secure storage: Encryption keys are stored in platform secure storage (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore)

4. Cloud Backup and Google Drive

Cloud sync is optional. If enabled:

  • Vael stores encrypted data in your own Google Drive account
  • We do not have access to your Google Drive, encryption keys, or authentication credentials
  • You can disable cloud sync at any time; existing encrypted files remain on Drive until you delete them
  • Encryption key derivation uses PBKDF2 with 100,000 iterations. The strength of your passphrase determines security
  • If you forget your passphrase, encrypted data cannot be recovered

5. Google Sign-In

Vael uses Google Sign-In only to authenticate you for Google Drive access. We:

  • Do not store your Google password
  • Do not store long-lived authentication credentials on our servers (we have none)
  • Receive only your email, display name, and a Drive-scoped OAuth token
  • Store the OAuth token locally on your device only

6. No Financial Advice

Vael is a tracking and planning tool only. It does not provide financial advice.

Vael helps you:

  • Track accounts, transactions, budgets, and goals
  • Calculate financial metrics (net worth, spending trends, loan amortization)
  • Plan for future scenarios using deterministic math (not predictions)

Vael does not:

  • Recommend investments, insurance, or financial products
  • Predict future market behavior or returns
  • Provide tax or legal advice
  • Use artificial intelligence, machine learning, or statistical models to guide decisions

Always consult qualified financial, tax, or legal professionals before making significant financial decisions.
